Who I am as a student.

I was a student who struggled with consistency. In elementary I was more drawn to art than academics and often won awards in drawing, posters, and slogans. In high school, I lost confidence in art and became unmotivated in academics, skipping classes for computer games and almost getting expelled.

Everything changed when my grandmother urged me to take school seriously. I shifted my focus, worked hard on my ICT studies, and slowly improved. In college, I discovered my strength in programming, led my thesis group, and dedicated myself to learning and teaching my peers. I never aimed for awards, only to graduate and make my parents proud, but through perseverance and discipline, I unexpectedly finished as Cum Laude.

My first job shaped my growth.

My very first job was as a Tech Support, where I handled troubleshooting and fixing hardware and software issues. The company was in the food industry and not very focused on technology, so I proposed some automation, developed a website, and soon got promoted to Web Developer. Just two months later, I was promoted again to IT Business Analyst.

I became the go-to person for automation and solutions that made my coworkers’ tasks easier. Beyond the work, I also built strong friendships, which made the experience memorable. After a year, I had to part ways because a new opportunity came along.

What you really don't know about me

I’ve never been the quiet type. In fact, I was so talkative in elementary school that I was even awarded “Most Talkative.” I’m also a very straightforward person. So if you’re wondering why I sometimes seem quiet, here’s the truth: I get shy, and I take time to observe who I can get along with. Since being straightforward can sometimes upset people, I hold back until I know I can be myself around them. I also describe myself as having a “squammy personality” (like from the informal settlement vibe). Some people distance themselves because of that but honestly, I don’t give a “F-recious” care. I’m actually easy to get along with, as long as you’re willing to meet me halfway. A couple of fun facts: I don’t really like sweets (though I’ll eat them occasionally). I’m allergic to fur, so no cats or dogs for me but if I could, I’d love to have a crow as a pet. That way, it could “legally” steal money and bring it back to me.
